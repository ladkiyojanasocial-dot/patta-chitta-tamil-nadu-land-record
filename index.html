Patta Chitta Tamil Nadu Land Record – Complete Guide 2025

In Tamil Nadu, land ownership and property verification are governed by the state’s digital record system known as Patta Chitta. These land records are maintained by the Revenue Department of Tamil Nadu and are essential documents for proving legal ownership, resolving property disputes, and applying for loans. Through the online system, citizens can now access, verify, and download their Patta Chitta land record without visiting Taluk or Village offices.

What Is Patta?

A Patta is a legal document issued by the Government of Tamil Nadu that certifies ownership of a parcel of land. It contains crucial information such as the owner's name, survey number, subdivision number, tax details, and the exact area of the land. The Patta is issued by the Tahsildar’s office and serves as a permanent record of ownership.

The Patta number is unique to each land parcel and helps government authorities track ownership history. It is also required during land transactions, mutation processes, and when seeking compensation or loans against property.

What Is Chitta?

The Chitta document provides classification and usage details of land — whether it is “Nanjai” (wetland suitable for cultivation) or “Punjai” (dry land). It specifies the type of crops grown, irrigation methods, and current land utilization. Together, Patta and Chitta form the comprehensive land record for any property in Tamil Nadu.

The Chitta is particularly valuable for agricultural assessments and determining land revenue taxes. Earlier, both documents were issued separately, but since 2015, the government integrated them into a single digital database accessible through the eservices.tn.gov.in portal.

Why Patta Chitta Is Important

Land ownership disputes are common in India, and Tamil Nadu is no exception. The Patta Chitta record acts as legal proof that a particular person owns or cultivates a specific piece of land. It also helps:

  • Establish ownership during property registration or transfer
  • Prevent encroachments and unauthorized occupation
  • Secure loans using property as collateral
  • Ensure smooth land mutation after inheritance or sale
  • Assist government surveys and urban development planning

How to Check Patta Chitta Tamil Nadu Land Record Online

The Government of Tamil Nadu offers an easy-to-use online platform to verify Patta Chitta records through the official e-Services portal. Here’s how you can access it step by step:

  1. Visit the official Tamil Nadu e-services website at eservices.tn.gov.in.
  2. Click on “View Patta & FMB / Chitta / TSLR Extract”.
  3. Select your district, taluk, and village from the dropdown menu.
  4. Enter your survey number and subdivision number correctly.
  5. Click on “Submit” to view your Patta Chitta details.
  6. The screen will display land ownership details, classification, and area size.
  7. You can download or print this record for future reference.

Details Found in Patta Chitta Records

A typical Patta Chitta record contains the following data:

  • Owner’s name
  • Survey number and subdivision
  • Patta number and land classification (Nanjai/Punjai)
  • Tax details and extent of the property
  • District, Taluk, and Village name
  • Corresponding FMB sketch or map reference

FMB Map and A-Register Extract

The Field Measurement Book (FMB) contains detailed sketches of land parcels, showing exact boundaries and neighboring plots. It is particularly useful for land disputes, boundary verification, and legal claims.

Similarly, the A-Register extract provides information about land classification, ownership, and extent. Together, these records give a complete view of a property’s legal and physical characteristics.

Patta Transfer Procedure

Transferring a Patta is required whenever land ownership changes — through sale, inheritance, gift, or partition. To transfer your Patta:

  • Submit an application at the respective Taluk office or online via e-services.
  • Attach sale deed, encumbrance certificate, and previous Patta.
  • Revenue officials verify ownership and update the records.
  • Once approved, the new Patta will be issued in the transferee’s name.

Common Reasons for Patta Chitta Discrepancies

Errors in Patta Chitta records can occur due to outdated manual entries, mismatched survey numbers, or unrecorded property transfers. If discrepancies arise:

  • Visit your local Taluk or Village Administrative Office (VAO).
  • Submit a correction request with supporting documents.
  • Follow up through the online grievance redressal system if delays occur.

Offline Verification of Patta Chitta

Citizens who prefer offline verification can still obtain Patta Chitta extracts by visiting the nearest Taluk office. Carry your Aadhaar card, property tax receipt, and sale deed copy. The officer will retrieve the record and provide a printed extract for a nominal fee.

Legal Uses of Patta Chitta

Patta Chitta records serve as critical legal documents in:

  • Property registration and title verification
  • Resolving boundary disputes or encroachments
  • Court proceedings regarding land ownership
  • Government compensation during land acquisition
  • Verification of agricultural subsidies and crop insurance claims

Digitization and Modernization

The Tamil Nadu government has made significant progress in digitizing its land records. Through initiatives like Digital India Land Records Modernization Programme (DILRMP), all records are being integrated into GIS-based mapping systems. This ensures transparency, reduces corruption, and allows citizens to verify ownership without intermediaries.

Future of Land Records in Tamil Nadu

The integration of blockchain technology and satellite mapping is expected to enhance accuracy and prevent manipulation in land records. Efforts are also underway to link Patta Chitta with Aadhaar and property tax databases for seamless verification. In the coming years, citizens will likely access a unified land information portal for all records.

Conclusion

The Patta Chitta Tamil Nadu land record system represents a major leap toward transparent and efficient land administration. It empowers property owners with easy access to verified records, eliminates dependency on intermediaries, and ensures accountability in governance. Whether you are buying land, resolving disputes, or simply verifying ownership, checking your Patta Chitta record online is the most reliable way to ensure legal clarity. For more details and simplified guides, visit the official portal at patta-chitta.org.
